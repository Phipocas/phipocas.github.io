Timecode (Performance)

The logistics of modern society pressure us to believe that there is a way to deal with time. That time is money. Therefore, it generates an initial problem, for which only its system has the solution. In this way, step by step, it makes human dependence on its organisms, institutions and devices grow. The construction of tools is not followed by a conscious social and emotional education, causing the technology that we have at our disposal to become, many times, a barrier to our own development. It takes more and more space, not only in the production processes, but also in those of creation, diluting the separation between our time and the experimentation processes. What do we save when we choose not to participate in a production process? What kind of consequences do these shortcuts carry outside of the current social perception of time? What problems can this create within the context of artistic diversity and authenticity?

This project is born from the need to stop this chain of thought, to prioritize individual processes and the construction of the individual in a society ruled by stress. The installation addresses, initially, the perception of individual time and, secondly, with a performance extension, the domestication of humans by their production and creation systems.
